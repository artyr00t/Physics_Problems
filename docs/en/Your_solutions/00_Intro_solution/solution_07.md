## 7. Logic & Series

This is a classic "trick" problem. It looks like you need to calculate an infinite number of short trips for the fly, but there is a much simpler way to think about it: focus on **time**.

### Step 1: How long does the trip last?

The fly can only fly as long as the bicycle is moving. Once the bicycle hits the wall, the fly is crushed and stops.

* The bicycle is **10 meters** away.
* It moves at **1 m/s**.
* **Time = Distance / Speed**
* $10 \text{ m} / 1 \text{ m/s} = \mathbf{10 \text{ seconds}}.$

### Step 2: How far does the fly go in that time?

We know the fly is flying at a constant speed of **2 m/s** for that entire duration. It doesn't matter how many times it turns around; its speed remains the same.

* **Distance = Speed $\times$ Time**
* Fly's Speed = $2 \text{ m/s}$
* Total Time = $10 \text{ seconds}$
* $2 \text{ m/s} \times 10 \text{ s} = \mathbf{20 \text{ meters}}.$

**The Answer:** The fly travels a total distance of **20 meters**.
