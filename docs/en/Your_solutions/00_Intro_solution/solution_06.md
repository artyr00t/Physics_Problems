## 6. Function Analysis

Function analysis helps us find the "peaks" (maxima) and "valleys" (minima) of a curve. To do this, we use **calculus** to see where the slope of the function becomes perfectly flat.

Our function is: 

$$f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7$$

### Step 1: Find the Derivative

The derivative, $f'(x)$, tells us the slope of the line at any point.

* The derivative of $3x^2$ is $6x$.
* The derivative of $-12x$ is $-12$.
* The derivative of the constant $7$ is $0$.
* **$f'(x) = 6x - 12$**

### Step 2: Find the "Flat" Point

A maximum or minimum happens where the slope is zero. We set the derivative to $0$:

* $6x - 12 = 0$
* $6x = 12$
* **$x = 2$**

### Step 3: Determine if it is a High or Low point

We look at the "Second Derivative" ($f''(x)$), which is the derivative of $6x - 12$.

* $f''(x) = 6$
* Because $6$ is a positive number, it means the curve is "smiling" (concave up). This tells us our point is a **local minimum**.

### Step 4: Find the $y$ value

Plug $x = 2$ back into the original equation to find the height:

* $f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7$
* $f(2) = 12 - 24 + 7 = -5$

**The Answer:** There is a **local minimum at the point (2, -5)**.
