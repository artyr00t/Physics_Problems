## 3. Proportionality

This problem uses the **Universal Law of Gravitation**, but you don't need to know complex physics to solve it. It is actually a lesson in how changing one part of a fraction changes the result.

The formula is: 

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

Think of it like a recipe. We are changing the "ingredients" (masses and distance) to see how the "final dish" (Force, $F$) changes.

### The Changes

1. **The Masses ($m_1$ and $m_2$):** Both are **halved**.
* Since they are on the top of the fraction (the numerator), making them smaller makes the force smaller.
* $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}$. So, the force is now **4 times weaker** just because of the masses.


2. **The Distance ($r$):** This is **doubled**.
* The distance is on the bottom (the denominator) and it is **squared** ($r^2$).
* If you double the distance ($2$), the effect is $2^2 = 4$.
* Because this is on the bottom, increasing the distance makes the force **4 times weaker** again.



### The Final Calculation

To find the total change, we multiply the effects together:

* Effect from masses: $\frac{1}{4}$
* Effect from distance: $\frac{1}{4}$
* Total change: $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{16}$

**The Answer:** The gravitational force $F$ becomes **$1/16$** of its original strength. If you move things twice as far away and make them half as heavy, the pull between them drops significantly.
