**8. Circular Motion**

**Calculate the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator. The Earth's radius is approximately 6378 km.**

---

**Step 1: Understanding the Situation**
Even though you feel like you are standing still, you are actually spinning very fast because the Earth is rotating. This is called **Circular Motion**. 
To find the "Centripetal Acceleration" ($a_c$), which is the invisible pull keeping you moving in a circle, we need two things:
* **Radius ($R$):** $6378 \text{ km} = 6,378,000 \text{ meters}$.
* **Time ($T$):** How long does it take for the Earth to spin once? Exactly **24 hours**.



**Step 2: Finding the Speed ($v$)**
First, let's see how fast you are actually moving. The distance around the Earth (Circumference) is $2\pi R$.
* **Distance:** $2 \times 3.14 \times 6,378,000 \approx 40,053,840 \text{ meters}$.
* **Time in seconds:** $24 \text{ hours} \times 3600 \text{ seconds} = 86,400 \text{ seconds}$.

Now, divide Distance by Time:
* **Speed ($v$):** $40,053,840 / 86,400 \approx \mathbf{463.6 \text{ m/s}}$. (That's faster than a jet plane!)

**Step 3: Calculating Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$)**
The formula for this "circular pull" is:
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{R}$$

Let's put our numbers in:
* **Calculation:** $a_c = \frac{(463.6)^2}{6,378,000}$
* **Calculation:** $a_c = \frac{214,924.96}{6,378,000}$
* **Result:** $a_c \approx \mathbf{0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2}$



**Step 4: Using the Alternative Formula (The "Omega" Way)**
You can also solve this using the rotation speed ($\omega$):
* **$\omega$:** $2\pi / 86,400 \approx 0.0000727 \text{ rad/s}$.
* **Formula:** $a_c = \omega^2 \cdot R$
* **Result:** $(0.0000727)^2 \times 6,378,000 \approx \mathbf{0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2}$.

**Conclusion:**
The acceleration is very small (about **$0.034 \text{ m/s}^2$**). Compared to gravity ($9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$), it is almost nothing, which is why you don't feel like you're being flung off into space!
