**9. Momentum Comparison**

**Which has greater momentum: a 2-gram fly flying at 10 m/s or a 60-gram tennis ball moving at 1 m/s?**

---

**Step 1: What is Momentum? ($p$)**
Think of momentum as "how hard it is to stop an object." It depends on two things:
1.  **Mass ($m$):** How heavy the object is.
2.  **Velocity ($v$):** How fast it is moving.

The formula is very simple: Just multiply them!
$$p = m \cdot v$$



**Step 2: Fixing the Units (The "Grams to Kilograms" Trap)**
In physics, we always use **Kilograms (kg)**. If we use grams, our answer will be wrong. 
* **1 kilogram = 1,000 grams**.
* **Fly:** $2\text{ g} = 2 / 1,000 = \mathbf{0.002\text{ kg}}$
* **Tennis Ball:** $60\text{ g} = 60 / 1,000 = \mathbf{0.06\text{ kg}}$

**Step 3: Calculating the Fly's Momentum**
* **Mass:** $0.002\text{ kg}$
* **Speed:** $10\text{ m/s}$
* **Calculation:** $0.002 \times 10 = \mathbf{0.02\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$

**Step 4: Calculating the Tennis Ball's Momentum**
* **Mass:** $0.06\text{ kg}$
* **Speed:** $1\text{ m/s}$
* **Calculation:** $0.06 \times 1 = \mathbf{0.06\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$



**Step 5: Comparing the Results**
Now we just look at the two numbers:
* **Fly:** $0.02$
* **Tennis Ball:** $0.06$

**Conclusion:**
The **tennis ball** has greater momentum ($0.06 > 0.02$). Even though the fly is moving much faster, the tennis ball is so much heavier that it wins the "hard to stop" contest!
