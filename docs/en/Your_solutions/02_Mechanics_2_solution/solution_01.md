**1. Gravitational Dependence**

**A simple pendulum has a period of 4 seconds on Earth. What would its period be on the Moon, where the gravitational acceleration is about 1/6th of Earth's? What is the required length of a simple pendulum to have a period of exactly 1 second on Earth?**

---

**Step 1: The Rule of the Swing (The Formula)**
A pendulum is just a weight hanging on a string. How long it takes to swing back and forth (the **Period**, $T$) depends on only two things:
1.  **Length ($L$):** The longer the string, the slower it swings.
2.  **Gravity ($g$):** The stronger the gravity, the faster it swings.

The "Magic Formula" is: $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$



**Step 2: Pendulum on the Moon**
Gravity on the Moon is much weaker ($1/6$ of Earth). 
* Since gravity ($g$) is at the bottom of the fraction, **smaller gravity** means a **longer time**.
* Because $g$ is inside a square root, if gravity is $6$ times smaller, the time ($T$) gets $\sqrt{6}$ times bigger.

**Calculation:**
* $T_{moon} = T_{earth} \times \sqrt{6}$
* $T_{moon} = 4 \times 2.45 \approx \mathbf{9.8 \text{ seconds}}$

**Simple logic:** On the Moon, gravity is weak and "lazy." It doesn't pull the weight down quickly, so the swing takes almost $10$ seconds instead of $4$.



**Step 3: Finding the Length for a 1-Second Swing**
Now we are back on Earth where gravity is $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. We want the swing to take exactly **$1$ second**. We need to find how long the string ($L$) should be.

1.  **Start with the formula:** $1 = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{9.8}}$
2.  **Move the $2\pi$:** Divide $1$ by $6.28$ (which is $2\pi$).
    * $1 / 6.28 \approx 0.159$
3.  **Remove the square root:** Square the number ($0.159 \times 0.159$).
    * $0.159^2 \approx 0.0253$
4.  **Find $L$:** Multiply that by the gravity ($9.8$).
    * $L = 0.0253 \times 9.8 \approx \mathbf{0.248 \text{ meters}}$

**Conclusion:**
* **On the Moon:** The same pendulum takes **$9.8$ seconds** to swing.
* **On Earth:** To get a perfect $1$-second swing, your string should be about **$25$ cm** long.
